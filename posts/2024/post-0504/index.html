Experiment May 4, 2024

Mans After Works

We build a new drone.
Experiment

We announce the start of construction for our new drone racing arena in the UTIAS dome. This development marks a significant milestone for our team, addressing the long-standing need for a larger and more convenient racing area. Previously, we relied on the motion capture room in the Learning Systems & Robotics Lab, which was too small and inconvenient for our growing needs. The new racing dome will provide a spacious flying and tracking area, tailored to our requirements.

In early April, we received permission from UTIAS to construct this arena, and we secured $5,000 funding from UTIAS, allocated to the University of Toronto Aerospace Team (UTAT). Construction began on April 30, 2024, after a rented dozer cleared the gravel to create a flat surface. Members from the FSC lab and UTAT collaborated on the construction, placing tarps to cover the sand, and purchasing necessary materials such as screws, zip ties, gloves, and PVC pipes for gate frames. We assembled one gate prototype on that day.

Today, May 5, 2024, we continued by shoveling remaining gravel, installed wood and steel frames, and mounted OptiTrack cameras around the area. These cameras provide millimeter-accurate positions and poses at up to 100Hz. We have covered the outer area with tarps to prevent sand intrusion, and the middle area with PVC foams for flatness, cleanliness, and protection in case of accidental crashes. The final arena offers a flying and tracking area of approximately 10m x 10m x 4m.

In the coming days, we will polish the setup, build four additional gates for a simple racing course, and calibrate the motion capture system. Our goal is to showcase drone flights in our brand-new racing dome during UTIAS’s 75th anniversary on May 10, 2024, to alumni, potential sponsors, and to foster future drone racing research.

ADR Wins Best Paper Award on UAVs at ICRA

Our paper “Time-Optimal Gate-Traversing Planner for Autonomous Drone Racing” won the prestigious Best Paper Award in ICRA 2024, held in Yokohama, Japan.
ICRA 2024 Best paper award Trajectory planning

We are thrilled to announce that our paper, “Time-Optimal Gate-Traversing Planner for Autonomous Drone Racing,” has won the IEEE ICRA Best Paper Award in Unmanned Aerial Systems at the International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) 2024, held in Yokohama, Japan. This prestigious recognition comes from a pool of over 4,000 submissions from researchers around the world, highlighting the significance and innovation of our research. The paper was authored by Chao Qin, a PhD student in the Flight Systems and Control (FSC) lab and the chief engineer of UTAT ADR, who proposed the core algorithm. He was joined by Maxime S.J. Michet and Jingxiang (Harry) Chen, undergraduate students at the University of Toronto Engineering Science, who respectively helped with real-world experiments and running baseline methods / plotting diagrams, under the supervision of Prof. Hugh H.T. Liu.

The paper focuses on planning time-optimal trajectories for quadrotor drones navigating through gates of various shapes, such as planar polygons, balls, and irregular 3D forms, while balancing time efficiency and computational cost. The algorithm employs adjustable polynomial splines and constraint elimination using diffeomorphism. In our results, we demonstrated that our method not only handles diverse gate shapes but also significantly reduces planning time while maintaining time optimality. For instance, on a simple track with 47 ball gates, the baseline method Complementary Progress Constraints (CPC) took nearly 8 hours to plan a trajectory with a duration of 44.01 seconds, whereas our method completed the planning in just 3.49 seconds, achieving a trajectory duration of 43.25 seconds, outperforming the baseline method.

Each co-author will receive a certificate and a total of $1,000 USD award. We extend our gratitude to the ICRA Unmanned Aerial Vehicle program co-chairs Prof. Davide Scaramuzza and Prof. Angela Schoellig, the ICRA reviewing committee, and support from UTIAS and the University of Toronto Faculty of Applied Science and Engineering, along with funding from Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC). This achievement underscores the importance of our work in advancing drone racing technology and opens up promising avenues for future research and innovation in autonomous aerial systems.
